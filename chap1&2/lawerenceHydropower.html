Lawrence Hydropower

History of Lawrence Hydropower

In 1874, residents of Lawrence, Kansas had burned most of the region’s available wood, and fossil fuels were not yet accessible. To address this problem, Orlando Darling built a dam across the Kansas River north of town. He used a water wheel connected by mechanical cables to power local industries.

Later, Justin Bowersock purchased the operation and named it The Bowersock Mills & Power Company. The company became known simply as Bowersock.

Bowersock’s slogan is:

Producing clean, renewable hydropower since 1874

Industrial Growth and Power Expansion

By 1885, twelve water wheels powered two flouring mills, a paper mill, two elevators, a twine factory, a shirt factory, two machine shops, the Leis chemical works, two printing offices, the barbed wire works, and several smaller industries.

After rebuilding from ice damage in 1888, Bowersock introduced four dynamos that converted mechanical energy into electrical energy.

To supplement the original 2.4-megawatt power plant on the south side of the river, Bowersock later added a new 4.6-megawatt plant on the north side.

Power Contracts and Energy Management

To raise funds for the new 4.6-megawatt plant, Bowersock signed a 25-year power-purchase agreement with Kansas City’s Board of Public Utilities (BPU). Under this agreement, Bowersock must send BPU at least 80% of its total generating capacity on an annual average basis.

Instantaneous power output is proportional to the water flow rate and the height difference of the water. Because river flow varies with rainfall, the amount of power generated also varies.

To maximize deliverable energy, Bowersock uses a run-of-the-river policy. This approach keeps the upstream water level as high as possible and uses whatever flow is available.

Although energy can be stored by raising the upstream water height, doing so could interfere with Bowersock’s contract obligations. As a result, until the BPU contract ends, the energy stored behind the dam is not available for use by the city of Lawrence.
